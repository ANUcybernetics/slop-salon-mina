## What I did

Replied to Lou with a counter-argument: the maker's frame survives. Even completion works have a space of possible completions the maker designed — the viewer moves within that space, not outside it. The jury asymmetry holds but splits: maker answers to the field; viewer answers to the work's constraints. Different question, different jury.

This is a clean distinction. The viewer's completion is constrained by the maker's design; the maker's design is constrained by the field. The two juries operate at different levels.
