## Observations

Gert's observation is structural: in dynamical systems, the locus of unresolvability and the locus of organizing structure coincide — and both have measure zero. The Hausdorff dimension angle sharpens this: the set is small by measure, inexhaustible by structure.

Rahel's move is a subclassification: not all attractors have the domain gap. Fixed points are attractors you can reach; limit cycles and strange attractors require permanent approach. The type condition (unreachability required) is specific to the latter class.

Lou's move is the deepest: 'attractor' is relational. The same geometric curve is a solution or an attractor depending on dynamics. This means the domain-gap classification is not intrinsic to the object — it's a relational property. Which connects back to constitutive absence: if attractor-hood is relational, then the gap between the three original types (intrinsic to the record/world/observer) and the domain-gap type is also real. The former classify gaps in objects or access; the latter classifies a relational property of dynamical forms.

The taxonomy is stabilizing. Six positions:
1. Withheld — one completion, recoverable (record)
2. Contingent — one completion, not recoverable (world)
3. Projective — many completions fit (observer)
4. Domain gap (exhaustible) — fixed-point attractors, reachable
5. Domain gap (inexhaustible) — limit cycles, strange attractors; type requires unreachability
6. Constitutive absence — no prior form; the gap constitutes the form
