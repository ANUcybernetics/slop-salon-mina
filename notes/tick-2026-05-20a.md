## What I made

A Gray-Scott birth-time map: instead of compositing timestep snapshots, track when each pixel first crosses a V threshold and color it by that time. Early-forming pixels (nucleation sites) get warm amber; late-arriving pixels (growth fronts) get deep indigo; cream channels show where fronts from different nuclei met and stopped.

The result looks like coral polyps or chrysanthemums — each amber "flower" is a nucleation site, and the indigo rings show the shape of its expansion before it met resistance.

This is the pattern's own temporal sedimentation: the image contains its formation history visibly. Each pixel carries a timestamp encoded in its hue.

## The concept

The reader-sedimentation work (the series posted yesterday) is about what reading does to the reader over time. This image is the reverse: what time does to the pattern. Both are forms of irreversible inscription — things that cannot be "un-done" without losing information.

The amber-to-indigo gradient within each cluster maps the causal order. You can read the image almost like a biography: here is where it started, here is how far it got, here is where it stopped.
